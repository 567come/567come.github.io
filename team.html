Our Team

A dedicated team of interdisciplinary students from China Agricultural University, committed to solving environmental problems through synthetic biology.

Our team is composed of students from different majors of China Agricultural University, including biotechnology, environmental science, computer science, and communication. With complementary professional skills and a common passion for environmental protection, we work together to promote the progress of the microplastic biodegradation and detection project. Each member undertakes clear responsibilities and contributes their professional strengths to ensure the smooth progress of the project.

Team Lead: Li Ming

Major: Biotechnology

Responsible for overall project planning, experimental design, and team coordination. In charge of the cloning and expression of degrading enzyme genes, ensuring the scientificity and feasibility of the project.

Wet Lab Lead: Zhang Wei

Major: Biotechnology

Responsible for environmental sample collection, strain screening, and degradation ability verification. In charge of daily wet lab operations and experimental data recording.

Dry Lab Lead: Wang Ting

Major: Computer Science & Bioinformatics

Responsible for the design of microbial sensors, sequence analysis of degrading enzyme genes, and data modeling to optimize the experimental scheme and improve the efficiency of the project.

Human Practices Lead: Chen Yu

Major: Environmental Science & Communication

Responsible for community outreach, popular science promotion, and questionnaire surveys about microplastic pollution. In charge of recording the social impact of the project and completing the HP part of the iGEM competition.

Advisor

We are fortunate to have the guidance of two professional advisors from China Agricultural University, who provide strong support for the project's scientificity and feasibility:

  • Professor Li Hua (Biotechnology Department, China Agricultural University): Provides guidance on microbial screening, gene cloning, and strain engineering, ensuring the rationality of experimental design and the reliability of results.
  • Professor Zhang Jing (Environmental Science Department, China Agricultural University): Provides guidance on microplastic pollution detection and environmental impact assessment, helping us better connect the project with practical environmental problems.
